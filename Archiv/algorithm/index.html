Task

Our first assignment in the course was to create an “analog algorithm.” This meant we had to come up with a graphic image in our minds and then write an instruction that explained, as precisely and clearly as possible, how the image should be drawn.

This instructions—the algorithm—was then given to another person, who tried to recreate the image based solely on our description. In this way, we could easily see which parts of our instructions were unclear or led to misunderstandings. After that, the algorithm was revised and refined and given to another person to test again. This process was repeated until we were satisfied with the outcome.

Personally, I approached this task by giving more and more detailed instructions step by step, making sure that the basic idea behind my graphic was clearly communicated.

Through this exercise, we learned the importance of giving clear and precise instructions—something that is essential in programming, since computers can only do exactly what you tell them to do.
